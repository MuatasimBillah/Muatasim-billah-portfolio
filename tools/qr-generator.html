Everything You Need to Know About QR Codes

QR (Quick Response) codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can store various types of information such as URLs, text, contact details, and more. They can be scanned using a smartphone camera or a QR code reader app, making them a versatile tool for businesses and personal use.

Tips for Creating Effective QR Codes

  • Keep data brief: Short URLs or text create less complex QR codes that are easier to scan and have higher success rates
  • Contrast matters: Ensure strong contrast between your QR code and background colors for better scannability in various lighting conditions
  • Size considerations: When printing QR codes, make sure they're at least 2×2 cm (0.8×0.8 inches) for reliable scanning from normal distances
  • Test before sharing: Always verify your QR code works with multiple devices and scanning apps before distributing it publicly
  • Error correction: Higher error correction makes your QR code more reliable but also more complex - balance based on your use case
  • Quiet zone: Leave white space (minimum 4 modules wide) around your QR code when placing it on marketing materials for optimal scanning
  • Avoid distortion: Maintain the square aspect ratio when resizing QR codes to ensure they remain scannable
  • Consider placement: Position QR codes where they can be easily scanned - avoid curved surfaces or areas with poor lighting

What is error correction in QR codes?

Error correction allows QR codes to be readable even when partially damaged or obscured. We offer four levels: Low (7%), Medium (15%), Quartile (25%), and High (30%). The percentage indicates how much of the QR code can be damaged while still remaining scannable. Higher levels make your QR code more reliable but also more complex, resulting in a denser pattern of modules.

Which file format should I download my QR code in?

We offer two download formats: PNG and SVG. PNG is ideal for direct use on websites, social media, or simple printing. SVG is a vector format that can be resized without losing quality, making it perfect for professional printing, graphic design projects, or applications where the QR code might need to be displayed at various sizes.
