Pro Tips for Perfect Results

Choose the Right Speed

Different genres require different slow-down rates. Pop and R&B usually sound best between 75-80%, while hip-hop works better at 70-75%. Use our genre recommendations for guidance.

Balance Your Reverb

Too much reverb can muddy the vocals, while too little won't create that atmospheric effect. For most tracks, 60-70% reverb creates the perfect balance of clarity and dreaminess.

Pick the Right Songs

Songs with clear vocals, emotional melodies, and defined bass lines transform best with slowed & reverb. Tracks that are already heavily processed or very dense may not yield dramatic results.

Use Bass Boost Wisely

A slight bass boost (2-4dB) enhances the dreamy quality, but too much can cause distortion. For tracks with heavy bass already, consider using less boost or none at all.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is slowed & reverb and why is it popular?

Slowed & reverb is an audio effect technique that reduces playback speed and adds spatial reverb to create a dreamy, atmospheric sound. It became viral on platforms like TikTok and YouTube because it transforms familiar songs into emotionally evocative experiences, perfect for aesthetic videos and relaxation content.

The effect creates a sense of nostalgia and depth that enhances the emotional impact of the music, making it ideal for short-form content that aims to create a specific mood or vibe.

What settings work best for different music genres?

For pop and R&B tracks, we recommend 75-80% speed with medium reverb (50-60%). Hip-hop and rap usually benefit from 70-75% speed with heavier reverb (60-70%). Electronic music works well at 80-85% with moderate reverb and longer decay.

For acoustic and instrumental tracks, try 75% speed with subtle reverb (40-50%) to preserve clarity. Our tool offers genre-specific recommendations to help you achieve the best results.

What are the optimal settings for viral TikTok slowed & reverb effects?

For TikTok-ready slowed & reverb effects that tend to go viral, we recommend using 75% speed, 65% reverb, 3-4 seconds decay, and a slight bass boost (+3dB). These settings create the perfect balance of dreaminess and clarity that performs well on social media.

Our 'TikTok Viral' preset automatically applies these optimized settings for you.

Can I use the processed audio in my social media content?

While our tool enables you to create slowed & reverb versions, please remember that the original copyright of songs remains with the artists and rightsholders. For personal use, there are typically no issues.

For commercial or monetized content, you should obtain proper licenses for the original music. Many creators use these effects for personal content on platforms like TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

Why do some songs sound better with slowed & reverb than others?

Songs with clear vocals, emotional lyrics, and simpler instrumentation often yield the best results with slowed & reverb effects. Tracks with strong melodies, defined bass lines, and atmospheric elements enhance the dreamy quality.

Dense, already-processed tracks with heavy compression may not transform as dramatically. Our tool's genre recommendations help you find the sweet spot for any track.
