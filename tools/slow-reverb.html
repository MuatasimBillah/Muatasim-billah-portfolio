What is 'Slow & Reverb' and why is it popular?

Slow & Reverb is an audio effect that slows down music and adds atmospheric reverb to create a dreamy, immersive sound. It became popular through genres like chopped and screwed, vaporwave, and phonk music, and has gained massive popularity on platforms like TikTok, YouTube, and SoundCloud. The effect creates a relaxing, nostalgic feeling that many listeners find appealing for relaxation, study sessions, or creating a certain mood.

Tips for the Perfect Slow & Reverb Effect

01

Choose the Right Source Material

Songs with clear vocals, distinct melodies, and not too many overlapping instruments work best for slow & reverb effects. R&B, pop, and hip-hop tracks are often excellent choices.

02

Find the Sweet Spot for Tempo

Most classic slow & reverb effects use between 70-80% of the original tempo. Too slow can sound unnatural, while too fast won't create the desired dreamy effect.

03

Balance Reverb and Clarity

While reverb creates atmosphere, too much can muddy the sound. Start with moderate settings and adjust until you find the perfect balance between ambience and clarity.

04

Use Bass Boost Sparingly

A slight bass boost (+3 to +6 dB) can enhance the slow & reverb effect, especially for hip-hop tracks, but too much can overpower other elements of the music.

05

Experiment with Different Presets

Try our different presets to quickly find a starting point that matches the mood you're trying to create, then fine-tune from there for the perfect sound.
